Theft charges in California cover a wide spectrum — from a $50 shoplifting incident to a multi-million-dollar embezzlement. Each level carries different bail amounts, custody locations, and long-term consequences. This guide explains the four most common LA theft charges, what bail to expect, and the LA-specific booking and court process.

LA Bail Amounts by Theft Charge

  • PC §490.2 / §484 petty theft (misdemeanor) — $500-$5,000
  • PC §459.5 shoplifting (misdemeanor) — $500-$5,000

  • PC §459 second-degree burglary (commercial) — $20,000 misdemeanor, $50,000 felony
  • PC §459 first-degree burglary (residential) — $50,000-$100,000
  • PC §211 robbery — $100,000-$250,000+

Why Burglary Is Different

Burglary under PC §459 does NOT require actual theft. The crime is entering with intent to commit theft or any felony. Prosecutors prove intent through circumstantial evidence — burglary tools, prior conduct, statements at the scene, or simply forced entry into a structure where the defendant has no business.

This matters for bail because: (1) burglary is charged independently of any successful theft, so charges can stack; (2) first-degree (residential) burglary is always a felony AND counts as a strike under California's Three Strikes Law (PC §667.5(c)(21)); (3) bail is set at the felony level even when nothing was actually stolen.

Robbery — Why It's the Most Serious

Robbery (PC §211) requires three elements: (1) taking property, (2) from another person or in their presence, (3) by force or fear. The "force or fear" element distinguishes robbery from theft — and triggers significantly harsher consequences. Robbery is always a felony, always a strike, and bail typically starts at $100,000.

LA Booking + Court Process

  1. Arrest by LAPD or LASD: Officers transport to local PD or sheriff's station for initial booking
  2. Booking: Fingerprints, photos, identity verification, charge entry. 2-8 hours.
  3. Transfer to LA County jails: Within 24-48 hours, most defendants transfer to Twin Towers Correctional Facility or Men's Central Jail (downtown LA). Female defendants transfer to Century Regional Detention Facility in Lynwood.
  4. Bail set: Misdemeanors per LA bail schedule; felonies typically require judge's hearing within 48 hours under CA PC §825.
  5. Bond posted: Angels Bail Bonds posts the surety bond. Call (626) 478-1062 24/7.
  6. Arraignment: Misdemeanors at local courthouse. Felonies — including all robbery and most first-degree burglary — at Clara Shortridge Foltz Criminal Justice Center, 210 W Temple St, LA.

Long-Term Consequences Beyond Custody

  • Strike convictions: First-degree burglary, robbery, and grand theft from a person count as strikes under PC §667.5(c) — affecting future sentencing
  • Firearm rights: Any felony conviction = federal lifetime firearm prohibition
  • Employment: Theft convictions appear on background checks and disqualify candidates for many financial-services, healthcare, and government jobs
  • Professional licensing: California Department of Real Estate, BRE, and Bureau of Cannabis Control all consider theft convictions in licensing decisions
  • Immigration: Theft convictions are "crimes of moral turpitude" — potentially deportable for non-citizens

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between petty and grand theft?
PC §490.2 petty theft = $950 or less, misdemeanor. PC §487 grand theft = over $950, wobbler. Some grand theft variants always felony (theft from a person, firearm, vehicle).
How much is theft bail in LA?
Petty theft $500-$5,000; grand theft $20K misdemeanor / $50K+ felony; first-degree burglary $50K-$100K; robbery $100K+. 10% premium per CA Ins Code §1800.4.
What is burglary under PC §459?
Entering any building, room, or locked vehicle WITH INTENT to commit theft or any felony. Actual theft not required. 1st-degree (residential) always felony + strike.
Can I bail out on theft?
Yes — bail available for nearly all theft charges per CA Constitution Art I §12. Misdemeanors set per schedule; felonies typically require judge's hearing within 48 hours.
Where are LA theft cases held?
Initial booking at local PD/LASD. Transfer within 24-48 hr to Twin Towers, Men's Central, or Century Regional. Felony arraignment at Foltz Criminal Justice Center.
