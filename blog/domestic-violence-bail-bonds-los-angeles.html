What Happens After a DV Arrest in Los Angeles County

Los Angeles County domestic violence arrests follow a consistent path from call to booking:

  • Officers respond and make an arrest. California's mandatory arrest law requires officers to take someone into custody when probable cause exists for a DV offense. This decision does not rest with the victim — once the call is made, the process is out of everyone's hands except the officer's.
  • Transport to booking. LAPD arrests within the City of Los Angeles typically go to Twin Towers Correctional Facility or Men's Central Jail, both located on Bauchet Street in downtown LA. LASD arrests in unincorporated areas or contract cities may go to a regional sheriff's station first, then transfer if needed.
  • Booking process begins. Booking at Twin Towers — the largest jail in the world by population — takes 4 to 12 hours depending on current intake volume. During this time, the defendant is photographed, fingerprinted, and their charge and bail amount are entered into the LASD inmate system.
  • Bail amount is set. The moment the charge is confirmed, bail is automatically set from the LA County Bail Schedule. No judge hearing is required for schedule bail. This is when 1-800-BAIL-911 starts working.

No mandatory hold in California: Domestic violence arrests in California do not carry a mandatory holding period. Bail posts from the county schedule as soon as booking is complete. Call 1-800-BAIL-911 the moment you hear about the arrest — we can begin paperwork before booking is even finished.

Twin Towers: The Jail That Never Sleeps

Twin Towers Correctional Facility processes more inmates than any other single jail facility in the United States. The complex includes two 10-story towers connected at the base, with specialized housing for medical, mental health, and general population inmates. Its sheer scale means that processing times can stretch, but they also mean the facility has a well-established bond posting protocol that experienced agents navigate every day.

Men's Central Jail at 441 Bauchet Street sits adjacent to Twin Towers and handles overflow from the intake process. Some DV defendants are processed at Men's Central rather than Twin Towers depending on booking volume at the time of intake. Both facilities accept bail bonds at their respective intake desks on a 24-hour basis.

Arraignment at the Clara Shortridge Foltz Criminal Justice Center

For defendants arrested and booked in central Los Angeles County, arraignment takes place at the Clara Shortridge Foltz Criminal Justice Center at 210 West Temple Street in downtown Los Angeles. The CJC, as it is commonly known, is the main criminal courthouse for LA County and handles the highest volume of DV arraignments in the state.

In-custody defendants are typically arraigned within 48 hours of arrest on court days (weekends and court holidays extend this window). At arraignment, the judge will review the charge, read the defendant their rights, and address bail — either confirming the schedule amount, raising it, or in rare cases reducing it. A criminal protective order will be issued in virtually every DV case at this hearing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a hold period after a domestic violence arrest in LA County?

No. California does not impose a mandatory hold period for DV arrests. Bail can be posted as soon as booking is complete and the bail amount is confirmed from the LA County schedule. The 72-hour hold claim is a myth.

How long does booking take at Twin Towers?

Typically 4 to 12 hours, depending on current intake volume. Twin Towers is the busiest intake facility in the country — volume fluctuates significantly. We monitor the system and post the bond the moment the booking appears, minimizing any additional delay on our end.

Can I call before booking is complete?

Yes — and you should. Call us as soon as you know someone has been arrested. We gather your information, prepare paperwork, and position ourselves to post the bond the moment it becomes available in the system. Every minute of preparation time is time saved on the back end.

What if the defendant has a prior DV arrest?

Prior DV arrests may trigger a bail enhancement under the LA County Bail Schedule. The judge at arraignment also has authority to raise bail above schedule amounts for repeat offenders. If prior history is a factor, call us immediately — we can review the situation and advise on realistic expectations before you commit to a payment plan.

What is the 10% premium on an LA County DV bail?

For a $50,000 bail (standard PC § 273.5 felony), the premium is $5,000. For a $100,000 bail (enhanced DV felony), the premium is $10,000. For a $10,000 misdemeanor bail, the premium is $1,000. These rates are fixed by California law and apply statewide.
